<objective>
Split the single `replays-fetcher` CronJob into two workloads on the user-approved
topology so new replays are ingested continuously (page-1 watch daemon) AND a
nightly full sweep catches anything that fell off page 1.

The watch-daemon code is already merged to replays-fetcher master and the image is
built; this plan only authors and offline-validates the Kubernetes manifests.

Image (both workloads, pinned exactly):
`ghcr.io/solid-stats/replays-fetcher:9f091bf1b913a2f04029b9b24ec8009130351495`

Purpose: continuous ingest latency (seconds, not 30-min cron windows) plus a
full-corpus nightly backstop, without losing the existing resilience tuning.

Output:
- `k8s/staging/52-replays-fetcher-watch.yaml` — new watch Deployment.
- `k8s/staging/50-replays-fetcher.yaml` — repurposed: shared SA + nightly run-once CronJob.

OUT OF SCOPE: live `kubectl apply`/deploy (orchestrator does that after merge);
replays-fetcher app code (already merged); any other manifest.
</objective>
